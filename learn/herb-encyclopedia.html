Herb Encyclopedia

Explore our comprehensive guide to medicinal plants. Learn about their uses, benefits, and much more.


1.Aloe Vera
• Scientific Name: Aloe barbadensis miller
• Description: Aloe Vera is a succulent plant known for its fleshy, gel-filled leaves. It is widely used in Ayurveda for its cooling, soothing, and moisturizing properties. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and has been used for thousands of years in traditional medicine.
• Uses:
1. Aloe Vera gel is applied topically to treat burns, cuts, and skin irritations due to its soothing and healing properties.
2. It is also consumed to aid digestion and improve gut health, offering relief from constipation and acidity.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Aloe Vera is recognized for its therapeutic benefits by AYUSH.


2.Amla (Indian Gooseberry)
• Scientific Name: Phyllanthus emblica
• Description: Amla is a small tree that produces greenish-yellow, sour fruits packed with Vitamin C. In Ayurveda, Amla is considered a powerful rejuvenator and is used in various formulations like "Triphala." It supports immune function and helps in overall well-being.
• Uses:
1. Amla boosts immunity and improves skin and hair health due to its antioxidant properties.
2. It helps in digestion, detoxifies the liver, and regulates cholesterol levels.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Amla is acknowledged for its wide range of health benefits.


3. Arjuna
• Scientific Name: Terminalia arjuna
• Description: Arjuna is a large evergreen tree, and its bark is highly valued in Ayurveda, particularly for heart health. Arjuna has been used for centuries to treat cardiovascular disorders and is known for its cardioprotective properties.
• Uses:
• It strengthens the heart muscles and is used to manage conditions like hypertension and angina.
• Arjuna also helps reduce cholesterol levels and supports overall cardiovascular health.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Arjuna is recognized for its heart health benefits.


4. Ashwagandha
• Scientific Name: Withania somnifera
• Description: Ashwagandha is a powerful adaptogen used to improve physical and mental stamina. It is a small shrub with yellow flowers, and its roots are the primary medicinal part. Ashwagandha helps the body resist stress and fatigue.
• Uses:
• Reduces anxiety and stress by lowering cortisol levels.
• Improves energy levels, vitality, and overall strength.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Ashwagandha is recognized for its adaptogenic properties.


5. Brahmi
• Scientific Name: Bacopa monnieri
• Description: Brahmi is a creeping herb known for its cognitive-enhancing effects. It has small succulent leaves and white flowers, and it thrives in wetlands. It is primarily used to boost brain function, including memory and concentration.
• Uses:
• Improves memory, focus, and cognitive function.
• Reduces anxiety and mental fatigue, promoting mental clarity.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Brahmi is recommended for its cognitive benefits.


6. Bilva (Bael)
• Scientific Name: Aegle marmelos
• Description: Bilva, also known as Bael, is a tree native to India, known for its fruit and medicinal properties. The fruit has a hard shell and is rich in nutrients. It is traditionally used in Ayurveda to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
• Uses:
• Supports digestive health and is effective in treating diarrhea and dysentery.
• Acts as a natural remedy for respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Bilva is recognized for its digestive and respiratory benefits.


7. Bhumi Amla (Phyllanthus)
• Scientific Name: Phyllanthus niruri
• Description: Bhumi Amla is a small herbaceous plant, often found in tropical regions. It is used in Ayurveda primarily for liver disorders and is known for its detoxifying and antiviral properties.
• Uses:
• Supports liver health and helps in managing conditions like jaundice and hepatitis.
• Acts as a natural diuretic and aids in detoxification.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Bhumi Amla is recognized for its liver-protective benefits.


8. Chitrak (Ceylon Leadwort)
• Scientific Name: Plumbago zeylanica
• Description: Chitrak is a perennial herb known for its fiery red flowers and its ability to stimulate digestion. It is primarily used in Ayurveda to balance Vata and Kapha doshas and improve digestive fire (Agni).
• Uses:
• Helps in treating indigestion, bloating, and other gastrointestinal issues.
• Acts as a natural stimulant for improving metabolism.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Chitrak is acknowledged for its digestive benefits.


9. Coriander (Cilantro)
• Scientific Name: Coriandrum sativum
• Description: Coriander, also known as cilantro, is an annual herb used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It is rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making it a staple in Ayurveda for promoting digestive health.
• Uses:
• Aids in digestion and helps in alleviating bloating and gas.
• Acts as a natural detoxifier, improving kidney and liver function.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Coriander is recognized for its digestive and detoxifying properties.


10. Daruharidra (Indian Barberry)
• Scientific Name: Berberis aristata
• Description: Daruharidra is a medicinal shrub used in Ayurveda primarily for treating skin and eye diseases. Its yellow-coloured root contains alkaloids that possess anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.
• Uses:
• Helps in treating skin conditions like acne, eczema, and infections.
• Supports eye health, and it is traditionally used to treat conjunctivitis.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Daruharidra is recognized for its skin and eye health benefits.


11. Gokshura (Puncture Vine)
• Scientific Name: Tribulus terrestris
• Description: Gokshura is a small, thorny plant known for its diuretic properties. It is used in Ayurveda to support urinary and reproductive health. The plant is also known for its ability to enhance physical strength and stamina.
• Uses:
• Supports urinary tract health by preventing kidney stones and improving kidney function.
• Enhances physical endurance and is often used as a natural supplement for athletes.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Gokshura is recognized for its diuretic and stamina-enhancing properties.


12. Guduchi (Giloy)
• Scientific Name: Tinospora cordifolia
• Description: Guduchi is a climbing shrub known for its immune-boosting properties. It is often referred to as "Amrita" in Ayurveda, symbolizing its ability to promote longevity and overall health. Guduchi is effective in balancing all three doshas.
• Uses:
• Enhances immunity and is commonly used to treat chronic fevers and respiratory infections.
• Detoxifies the body and supports liver function.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Guduchi is acknowledged for its immune-boosting and detoxifying effects.


13. Haritaki
• Scientific Name: Terminalia chebula
• Description: Haritaki is a small to medium-sized tree whose dried fruit is used extensively in Ayurveda. Known as the "King of Medicines," Haritaki is one of the components of the famous Ayurvedic formula "Triphala."
• Uses:
• Promotes digestion, improves bowel movements, and relieves constipation.
• Acts as a natural detoxifier and rejuvenator for the entire body.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Haritaki is recognized for its digestive and detoxifying benefits.


14. Jatamansi (Spikenard)
• Scientific Name: Nardostachys jatamansi
• Description: Jatamansi is a perennial herb that grows in the Himalayas. It is known for its calming and grounding effects, making it a popular herb in Ayurveda for improving mental health and relieving stress.
• Uses:
• Supports relaxation and mental clarity by reducing anxiety and promoting restful sleep.
• Helps in treating neurological conditions like epilepsy and hysteria.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Jatamansi is recognized for its calming and mental health benefits.


15. Kalmegh (Green Chiretta)
• Scientific Name: Andrographis paniculata
• Description: Kalmegh is a bitter herb used in Ayurveda primarily for its liver-protective and immune-boosting properties. It is a small annual plant that grows in tropical and subtropical regions, and its bitter taste reflects its ability to cleanse the body and purify the blood. Kalmegh is often called the "king of bitters."
• Uses:
• Supports liver health by detoxifying the liver and aiding in the treatment of liver disorders like hepatitis.
• Boosts immunity and is commonly used to treat infections, fevers, and the common cold.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Kalmegh is acknowledged for its liver-supporting and immune-boosting properties.


16. Kumari (Aloe Vera)
• Scientific Name: Aloe barbadensis miller
• Description: Kumari, also known as Aloe Vera, is a succulent plant recognized for its cooling and moisturizing properties. The gel inside the leaves is widely used in both external and internal applications. In Ayurveda, Kumari is used to balance Pitta dosha and promote skin, digestive, and reproductive health.
• Uses:
• Applied topically to treat burns, wounds, and skin irritations due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
• Taken internally to aid digestion and detoxify the body, helping alleviate conditions like constipation.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Aloe Vera is recognized for its skin-healing and digestive benefits.


17. Kutki
• Scientific Name: Picrorhiza kurroav
• Description: Kutki is a perennial herb that grows in the Himalayan region. It is highly valued in Ayurveda for its hepatoprotective and immune-modulating effects. Kutki is often used to treat liver and respiratory ailments and is known for its bitter taste.
• Uses:
• Supports liver health by helping treat liver diseases like jaundice and hepatitis.
• Acts as an anti-inflammatory and helps treat respiratory issues like asthma and bronchitis.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Kutki is recognized for its liver-supporting and anti-inflammatory properties.


18. Licorice (Mulethi)
• Scientific Name: Glycyrrhiza glabra
• Description: Licorice, or Mulethi, is a sweet-tasting root used in Ayurveda for its soothing effects on the respiratory and digestive systems. The plant contains glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties. Licorice is widely used to treat throat and stomach ailments.
• Uses:
• Soothes the respiratory tract and is commonly used to treat coughs, colds, and sore throats.
• Helps alleviate digestive issues like indigestion, ulcers, and acid reflux.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Licorice is recognized for its benefits to respiratory and digestive health.


19. Manjistha
• Scientific Name: Rubia cordifolia
• Description: Manjistha is a perennial climbing herb known for its blood-purifying properties. It has a long history of use in Ayurveda to detoxify the blood and treat skin disorders. Manjistha is often used in formulations to balance Pitta dosha and improve skin health.
• Uses:
• Purifies the blood and is commonly used to treat skin issues such as acne, eczema, and hyperpigmentation.
• Acts as a natural anti-inflammatory and helps reduce swelling and inflammation in the body.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Manjistha is recognized for its blood-purifying and skin-healing properties.


20. Musta (Nutgrass)
• Scientific Name: Cyperus rotundus
• Description: Musta, or Nutgrass, is a perennial herb used in Ayurveda to support digestive health. The plant grows in tropical regions and has tuberous roots that are used medicinally. Musta is particularly effective in balancing Pitta and Kapha doshas and promoting digestive fire (Agni).
• Uses:
• Supports digestion and helps treat conditions like diarrhea, bloating, and indigestion.
• Acts as a natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic, used to reduce pain and inflammation.
• Verified by AYUSH: Yes, Musta is recognized for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
